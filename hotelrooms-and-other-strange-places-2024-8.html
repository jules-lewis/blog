    Hotelrooms and Other Strange Places 2024: #8

    February 15, 2025 Jules
    CTF OSINT

    A search for art, or the art of search? Either.

    This is a walkthrough of an OSINT CTF set by The SEINT in a GitHub repo you can find here. You can see how the challenge works, and how I solved Step 1 at the start of the series.

    SPOILER ALERT!

If you want to have a go at solving The SEINT's puzzle yourself, please DON'T read on here, as this is obviously heavily spoilered.

    Step 8

    This puzzle was a lot of fun. We start off with this collage:

    The Seint then addes the following text:

    I really love nature, animals, old architecture, but to be honest, I can also enjoy some European art! During my latest travels, I also visited a famous museum, where I was fortunate to view some lovely paintings, which were all on display, and took some awesome photos. But somehow, after I managed to create a collage of some of the paintings, I lost the original ones! Can you help me find the original painters? Just the first two letters of their real first names are enough for me to be reminded. Just collect the eight characters, convert them to lower case, put them in alphabetical order, and hash them. So let’s say all of them were painted by Vincent van Gogh, the first two letters are “vi”, and in alphabetical order, all eight would be: “iiiivvvv”. Got it? Off you go!

    Google Lens

    Google Lens was a big help with some of these, but the bottom-right excerpt gave me a few problems. Just loading the unedited photo seems to indicate that Google evaluates images left-to-right, top-down. In any case, it identified the top left quadrant straightaway:

    Google Lens has a built-in cropping tool that does let you focus in on other areas of the image though. This segment also resulted in a clue to which gallery The Seint was referring to:

    This technique also got me the bottom-left segment, so with some further searches I had the following information:

    • top left: Leonardo da Vinci — Ginevra de Benci (1474)
    • top-right: Peter Paul Rubens — The Portrait of Marchesa Brigida Spinola-Doria (1606)
    • bottom-left: Antonio da Correggio — Salvator Mundi (1515)
    • bottom-right: ???

    I then got a lot of false positives searching on the bottom-right segment. I had no idea of the variety of earrings there are out the in the world of art! I also got no obvious results from Bing or Yandex.

    Eventually, I was able to move forward by actually cropping the original collage down to the bit I was interested in, and running it through TinEye:

    One of the results was a little larger than 474x568, but not by much. In any case, it was just large enough to run back through the various search engines…

    Google then confidently identified the wrong painting, but hinted at an artist that might need further searching:

    Clicking on that related search gives me a number of options, including a page on the National Gallery of Art website. Worth checking as this is the gallery showing the other works.

    Sure enough, a hit on one of the first paintings! I had to zoom in to verify, but you can just about make it out here:

    This completes the list, and I had the letters needed:

    • top left: Leonardo da Vinci — Ginevra de Benci (1474)
    • top-right: Peter Paul Rubens — The Portrait of Marchesa Brigida Spinola-Doria (1606)
    • bottom-left: Antonio da Correggio — Salvator Mundi (1515)
    • bottom-right: Nicolaes Maes — Portrait of a Lady (1676)

    Sorting these, turning them to lower-case, and hashing them was enough to get to Step 9!
